Upbringing

Pascal Siakam was born in Douala, Cameroon, in 1994. He grew up playing soccer and was introduced to basketball at the age of 15 by his older brothers. Siakam’s father passed away in a car accident when he was just two years old, and he and his five siblings were raised by their mother, who worked as a farmer to support the family. Siakam attended high school in the United States at God’s Academy in Lewisville, Texas, and later played college basketball at New Mexico State University. Despite his unconventional path to the NBA, Siakam has become a rising star in the league and an important player for the Toronto Raptors.

 

Pascal Siakam was a breakout star for the Toronto Raptors during their 2019 championship run. The young forward made significant contributions on both ends of the court and proved to be a valuable asset for the team. Siakam averaged 19.0 points, 7.1 rebounds, and 2.8 assists per game in the playoffs, showcasing his versatile skillset and ability to score in a variety of ways. He had several standout performances, including a 32-point outing in Game 1 of the NBA Finals, where he dominated the Golden State Warriors defence with his athleticism and scoring ability. Siakam’s energy and athleticism were vital for the Raptors throughout the postseason, and he played a crucial role in helping the team secure their first NBA championship.
